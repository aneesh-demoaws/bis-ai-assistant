ğŸ“ Key Concepts Explained

ğŸ¤– What is Generative AI?

Generative AI is like a super-smart assistant that can create new content - it can write text, have conversations, and answer questions just like a human would. Think of it as a robot that learned by reading millions of books and can now help you with almost anything!

ğŸ§  What is an LLM (Large Language Model)?

An LLM is the "brain" of the AI assistant. It's a computer program trained on huge amounts of text to understand and generate language. Just like you learned to speak by listening to people around you, the LLM learned by "reading" billions of sentences from books, websites, and articles.

ğŸ“š What is RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)?

RAG is a smart technique that makes AI more accurate. Instead of just guessing answers, the AI first searches for real information from trusted sources (like your school's newsletters), then uses that information to create an answer. It's like doing research before answering a question in class - you get better, more accurate answers!

ğŸ¯ How Does BIS AI Assistant Use These?

When you ask a question:

  1. The LLM brain understands what you're asking
  2. It decides whether to search the School Knowledge Base or the Web
  3. Using RAG, it retrieves real information from these sources
  4. The LLM combines this information to generate a helpful, accurate answer
  5. You get the answer through text or voice!

ğŸ’¡ Why Is This Cool?

This AI assistant is special because it doesn't just make up answers - it actually looks up real information about BIS events, achievements, and activities. For general questions, it searches the internet to give you current, accurate information. It's like having a smart librarian who knows everything about your school and can also search the entire internet in seconds!
